# Sacred Seven — Psychology Audit

**Lens:** Pricing psychology, motivation triggers, conversion mechanics.

## What emotional trigger pushes the click?
Exclusivity and identity. The opener ("vous n'etes pas au bon endroit") is a **pattern interrupt** (Cialdini: Contrast) that filters out the curious and flatters the serious buyer. The visitor thinks: "I'm not like the others — I belong here." This is **identity-based motivation** (James Clear model). The luxury tone (copper, stamps, Paris/Dubai/Shanghai) activates **aspirational self-image**, not rational ROI calculation.

## Urgency, Scarcity, Social Proof, Loss Aversion?
- **Social proof:** Present but passive — Bollore, Seb, Nestle are name-dropped in body copy, not hero. No logos, no numbers, no quotes. Cialdini's Authority principle is hinted at, never landed.
- **Urgency/Scarcity:** Zero. No time constraint, no limited slots, no "X companies onboarded this quarter." The page is timeless — beautiful, but psychologically inert on this axis.
- **Loss aversion (Kahneman):** Absent. No framing of what the visitor loses by NOT acting. The page is 100% approach-motivated, 0% avoidance-motivated.

## Motivation model
Purely **intrinsic**. The page appeals to taste, discernment, vision. No extrinsic lever (price anchor, ROI figure, saved hours, competitive advantage). This works for ultra-luxury but is risky for B2B SaaS/consulting where the buyer must justify spend internally.

## Is "Planifier un entretien" the right CTA?
It is low-threat (Fogg: reduce Ability barriers) — no form, no commitment language. But it is also **zero-desire**. "Entretien" is clinical, almost administrative. It does not encode any **anticipated value**. Compare: "Decouvrir ce que l'IA change pour vous" (curiosity + personalization) or "Reservez votre diagnostic" (implies they GET something). The CTA currently asks for effort without promising reward — violating Fogg's B=MAP model (Motivation is too low relative to the Action requested).

## The ONE missing principle that would double conversions
**Concrete social proof with specificity** (Cialdini #4 + Kahneman's anchoring). Add one single line above or near the CTA: a specific, quantified result. Example: "47 entreprises accompagnees en 2025 — 3 mois de delai moyen entre diagnostic et production." This anchors credibility, triggers herd behavior, and gives the rational brain permission to act on what the emotional brain already wants. Without it, the page is a mood — not a decision architecture.
